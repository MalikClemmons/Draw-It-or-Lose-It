# Draw-It-or-Lose-It
Software Design and Architecture Portfolio Project
# Portfolio Reflection

**Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design?**

The client was The Gaming Room, a company that wanted to expand its game, *Draw It or Lose It*, from Android to a web-based distributed application. The software needed to support multiple teams and players while ensuring that only one game instance could exist at a time through a scalable and efficient design.

**What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?**

I believe I did well evaluating different operating platforms and comparing their strengths and weaknesses. This helped me make recommendations that aligned with the client's performance, scalability, and accessibility requirements.

**What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?**

The design document helped organize requirements before implementation began. By identifying system needs, constraints, and architecture decisions early, it created a clear roadmap for development.

**If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?**

If I could revise one section, I would improve the evaluation and recommendation section by providing more detailed comparisons between operating environments. This would strengthen the justification for the final recommendation.

**How did you interpret the user's needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user's needs when designing?**

I focused on the client's need for scalability, accessibility, and support across multiple platforms. Considering user needs is important because the software must solve the client's problem while providing a reliable and efficient experience for end users.

**How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?**

I approached the design process by gathering requirements, analyzing system constraints, evaluating architecture options, and documenting recommendations. In future projects, I would continue using requirement analysis, UML diagrams, and platform evaluations to ensure the software design meets both technical and business requirements.
